Humanwell Healthcare Group

👤 David Wang · Sr. BD Director
1
Executive Summary
▲

Humanwell Healthcare Group is a large-cap Chinese pharma company (revenue ~USD 3.3B, market cap ~USD 4.45B) with leading market positions in neurology drugs, particularly anesthesia and analgesia. The company's neurology portfolio spans narcotic analgesics, anesthetic agents, and recently approved anti-Parkinson's drugs, distributed through neurology departments across China's hospital network.

Under new ownership by China Merchants Group (2025), the company is focused on consolidating its neurology franchise and expanding into innovative drug development. Key subsidiaries include Yichang Humanwell (flagship innovative drug unit), Epic Pharma (US generics), and Paion AG (Germany, hospital sedation/critical care).

2
Pipeline / Business Model Introduction (NMD Focus)
▲

Humanwell is a fully-integrated Chinese pharma company (SSE: 600079) with dominant positions in China's anesthesia and analgesia market. Its core subsidiary Yichang Humanwell is Asia's largest designated R&D and production base for narcotic drugs, with neurology-related drug revenue reaching approximately RMB 75 billion in 2024 (+11%).

Key neurology-relevant products and pipeline include:

  • Marketed products: Remifentanil (market share ~90%), sufentanil, hydromorphone ER tablets, oxycodone ER tablets, esketamine injection, etomidate injection.
  • Recent approvals: Levodopa-benserazide tablets (anti-Parkinson's, March 2025). Market size >RMB 1B (2023), ~7% growth H1 2024.
  • Biologics pipeline — pUDK-HGF: Class 1 biologic (plasmid-based HGF gene therapy) for severe lower limb ischemia. NDA submitted Dec 2024, withdrawn Dec 2025 for supplementation; plans to resubmit.
  • Other CNS pipeline: ~60 R&D programs including Nav1.8 channel inhibitor (pain), tapentadol, hydrocodone/ibuprofen tablets.
3
Deals & Transaction Summary in NMD (Last 3 Years)
▲

No neurology/NMD-specific licensing or partnership deals identified in public sources.

4
Corporate Strategy on NMD
▲

None of Humanwell's stated strategic priorities include neuromuscular disease, regenerative medicine, cell/gene therapy for NMD, or rare disease. The closest thematic link to EN001 is Humanwell's broad "CNS" branding, but in practice this is limited to anesthesia and pain pharmacology.

Humanwell's stated strategy is to be a leader in pharmaceutical sub-segments, with neurology drugs as its primary strategic focus area alongside steroidal hormones and Uyghur medicines.

CMS (China Medical System)

👤 ZiYe Shen · BD Director
1
Executive Summary
▲

CMS is a specialty pharma commercialization platform (HKEX: 0867, SGX: 8A8) with 2024 turnover of RMB 7,469 million and a promotion network covering 50,000+ hospitals across China. CNS is a core therapeutic area, with active neurology products including VALTOCO (diazepam nasal spray for epilepsy seizures, NMPA-approved 2023) and ZUNVEYL (benzgalantamine for Alzheimer's, licensed from Alpha Cognition in Jan 2025, China NDA accepted July 2025). CMS also commercializes rare disease products including Tetrabenazine (Huntington's chorea).

As a pure licensing/commercialization platform with established CNS and rare disease regulatory track record, CMS represents a potential partner for EN001's China commercialization.

2
Pipeline / Business Model Introduction (NMD Focus)
▲

CMS is a pure commercialization platform (no manufacturing) listed on HKEX and SGX, with a promotion network covering over 50,000 hospitals and medical institutions and approximately 250,000 retail pharmacies in China, supported by approximately 4,400 professional academic promotional staff (as of end-2023). CNS is one of six core therapeutic areas. 2024 turnover was RMB 7,469 million.

Key neurology-relevant products and pipeline:

  • VALTOCO (Diazepam Nasal Spray): NMPA-approved June 7, 2023 for acute treatment of seizure clusters/acute repetitive seizures in epilepsy patients aged 6+. First diazepam nasal spray in China. Included in China's NRDL Category B effective January 2024. A real-world clinical study enrolling ~10,000 cases is underway in China.
  • ZUNVEYL (Benzgalantamine): Licensed from Alpha Cognition (Jan 2025, $44M total deal value including $6M upfront) for exclusive development and commercialization in Asia (excl. Japan), Australia, and New Zealand. FDA-approved July 2024 for mild-to-moderate Alzheimer's disease. NDA accepted by NMPA on July 28, 2025. Next-generation acetylcholinesterase inhibitor designed to reduce GI side effects.
  • Tetrabenazine Tablets: Rare disease drug for Huntington's disease chorea, newly included in NRDL Category B (2023 version), effective January 2024.
3

  • Jan 2025: Exclusive license from Alpha Cognition for ZUNVEYL (benzgalantamine) in Asia/Australia/NZ — $44M total deal value ($6M upfront + milestones), for Alzheimer's disease.
  • 2023: Licensed-in VALTOCO (diazepam nasal spray) — NMPA-approved and included in NRDL within the same year.

Roche China

👤 Sophie · BD Director
1
Executive Summary
▲

Roche is a global biopharmaceutical leader with 7,000 employees in China and 2024 China revenue of CHF 5.328 billion. Neurology is one of Roche's three strategic pillars (alongside oncology and cardiovascular-metabolic), with global neurology revenue growing 13% in 2024.

Roche has a significant neuromuscular disease portfolio anchored by Evrysdi (risdiplam, SMA) — approved in 100+ countries with 18,000+ patients treated — and an ex-US commercialization partnership with Sarepta Therapeutics for Elevidys (DMD gene therapy). The neurodegenerative pipeline includes trontinemab (Alzheimer's, Phase III), prasinezumab (Parkinson's), and fenebrutinib (MS). Roche has publicly stated it is seeking first-in-class or best-in-class molecules from Asian biotechs.

2
Pipeline / Business Model Introduction (NMD Focus)
▲

2024 China revenue was CHF 5.328 billion (pharma CHF 2.926B + diagnostics CHF 2.402B). Neurology is one of Roche's three shared priority disease areas, alongside oncology and cardiovascular-metabolic. Global neurology revenue grew 13% in 2024, driven mainly by Ocrevus, Evrysdi, and Elevidys.

NMD portfolio:

  • Evrysdi (risdiplam): Oral, non-invasive SMA therapy, approved in 100+ countries with 18,000+ patients treated globally (as of June 2025). Five-year SUNFISH data showed continued stabilisation of motor function in Types 2/3 SMA. US tablet formulation approved February 2025. Developed in collaboration with the SMA Foundation and PTC Therapeutics.
  • Elevidys (delandistrogene moxeparvovec): Gene therapy for DMD, developed by Sarepta Therapeutics; Roche responsible for ex-US markets. In June 2025, FDA issued a safety alert following two deaths in non-ambulatory patients (acute liver failure); non-ambulatory indication removed and Boxed Warning added November 2025. EMA issued negative opinion July 2025. Currently indicated only for ambulatory patients aged 4+ with confirmed DMD gene mutation.
  • Enspryng (satralizumab): Being studied for generalised myasthenia gravis (gMG).

Other neurodegenerative pipeline:

  • Trontinemab: Brain-penetrant anti-amyloid antibody for Alzheimer's disease. Phase III initiated in 2025.
  • Prasinezumab: Anti-alpha-synuclein antibody for Parkinson's disease.
  • Alogabat: For Angelman syndrome.
  • Fenebrutinib: Brain-penetrant BTK inhibitor for relapsing and progressive MS.
  • RG6289: Gamma-secretase modulator for Alzheimer's disease.
3
Deals & Transaction Summary in NMD (Last 3 Years)
▲
  • 2019 (ongoing): Collaboration with Sarepta Therapeutics for ex-US development and commercialization of Elevidys (DMD gene therapy).
  • 2025: Phase III Go decision for trontinemab in Alzheimer's disease. Zealand Pharma collaboration (pending closure).

Roche's NMD portfolio is primarily driven by internal pipeline and the Sarepta partnership.

4
Corporate Strategy on NMD
▲

Roche has explicitly positioned neurology — including neuromuscular disease — as one of three strategic pillars alongside oncology and cardiovascular-metabolic. The company's NMD strategy centers on Evrysdi (SMA franchise) and the Sarepta partnership for Elevidys (DMD). Global neurology revenue grew 13% in 2024.

Biogen

👤 Yves Wyckmans · International BD Lead
1
Executive Summary
▲

Biogen (NASDAQ: BIIB, Q1 2025 revenue $2.43B, +6% YoY) is undergoing a strategic transformation from a legacy MS company to a rare neuromuscular and neurological disease company. Its rare disease franchise — Spinraza (SMA), Skyclarys (Friedreich's ataxia, acquired via $7.3B Reata buyout), and Qalsody (SOD1-ALS) — generated $563M in Q1 2025 (+33% YoY), now representing ~45% of promoted product revenue.

The company actively pursues external rare disease assets through licensing and acquisition, with a global network of 45+ affiliates and distribution partners in over 60 additional countries.

2
Pipeline / Business Model Introduction (NMD Focus)
▲

NMD / Rare Disease portfolio:

  • Spinraza (nusinersen): First FDA-approved SMA therapy (December 2016). Antisense oligonucleotide, intrathecal injection. Became Biogen's best-selling drug in Q1 2025. FDA approved High Dose Regimen in March 2026 (DEVOTE study). Salanersen (next-gen SMA ASO) advancing to registrational stage.
  • Skyclarys (omaveloxolone): First and only approved treatment for Friedreich's ataxia, acquired via $7.3B Reata Pharmaceuticals buyout (Q4 2023). Q1 2025 global revenue ~$124M (+59% YoY), ~2,400 patients on therapy globally. Commercialized in 40+ countries. Pediatric Phase 3 BRAVE study (ages 2–16) initiated June 2025.
  • Qalsody (tofersen): ASO therapy for SOD1-ALS. Commercially available in the US and EU.

Rare disease revenue: $563M in Q1 2025 (+33% YoY), ~45% of promoted product revenue.

Neurodegenerative pipeline:

  • Leqembi (lecanemab): Anti-amyloid antibody for Alzheimer's (50/50 collaboration with Eisai). EU approval April 2025; US maintenance dosing approved January 2025.
  • Litifilimab: Anti-BDCA2 antibody, Phase 3 in SLE and cutaneous lupus (Breakthrough Therapy Designation).
3
Deals & Transaction Summary in NMD (Last 3 Years)
▲
  • 2023: Acquired Reata Pharmaceuticals for $7.3B — adding Skyclarys, first approved treatment for Friedreich's ataxia.
  • 2025: Agreement with Stoke Therapeutics for zorevunersen (Dravet syndrome, rare genetic epilepsy; pivotal Phase 3 expected to initiate). Acquired Alcyone Therapeutics (~$85M) for ThecaFlex DRx intrathecal delivery technology.
4
Corporate Strategy on NMD
▲

Biogen CEO has described the company as transforming from an MS company to a rare disease and neurology company. Rare disease now represents ~45% of promoted product revenue. The late-stage pipeline includes 10+ registrational studies expected to read out over the next four years.

5
Attendance with Title
▲

Yves Wyckmans — International Business Development Lead

6
Background of Our Approach
▲

EN001 as a modality extension to Biogen's neuromuscular disease franchise:

  • Same disease burden, zero competition: Like SMA and ALS, CMT is a progressive neuromuscular disorder — yet zero approved drugs exist globally for ~560,000 patients in China. EN001's validated repeat-dosing MSC therapy complements Biogen's ASO platforms by addressing chronic tissue repair and functional restoration.
  • Chronic management synergy: SPINRAZA's success is built on lifelong repeat dosing. EN001's validated repeat-dosing safety supports the same chronic disease management model, with potentially broader patient convenience (IV/IM vs. intrathecal).
  • Rare-to-mass-market pivot: CMT entry point (~560K China patients) → sarcopenia second indication (20.7% China 60+ prevalence, zero approved drugs globally). Biogen's global rare disease access network could unlock both markets.
  • De-risked, late-stage: Phase 1 completed with no SAEs, 6.2-point CMTNSv2 improvement reversing 10+ years of progression; Korea conditional approval pathway established.

HK WinHealth Pharma Group

👤 David Jiang · BD Director
1
Executive Summary
▲

Hong Kong WinHealth Pharma Group (private, founded 2006) is a China-based biopharmaceutical company focused on licensing-in and commercializing innovative therapies, with core operations in nephrology, hematology, and rare diseases. Headquartered in Hong Kong with hubs in Hangzhou, Shanghai, Beijing, and representative offices in Japan, Singapore, and Europe.

In 2024, WinHealth acquired 100% equity of Kyowa Kirin China (up to RMB 720M / ~US$104M), transforming the company into an integrated pharma enterprise. Key NMD-relevant assets: CRYSViTA® (burosumab) for X-linked hypophosphatemia; China's first rare disease Bo'ao Managed Access Platform (22+ overseas-approved orphan drugs); and a five-year strategic collaboration with China Alliance for Rare Diseases (CARD).

2
Pipeline / Business Model Introduction (NMD Focus)
▲
  • CRYSViTA® (burosumab): Recombinant fully human monoclonal antibody against FGF23, for X-linked hypophosphatemia (XLH) and tumor-induced osteomalacia (TIO). Long-term China commercialization rights obtained via license agreement with Kyowa Kirin (2024).
  • Bo'ao Managed Access Platform: Launched April 2020 at Hainan Bo'ao Lecheng International Medical Tourism Pilot Zone — China's sole "medical special zone" enabling compassionate use of overseas-approved but not yet NMPA-approved drugs. 22+ innovative rare disease drugs included, such as amifampridine (for Lambert-Eaton myasthenic syndrome, an NMD). Represents a potential early access pathway for EN001 prior to full NMPA approval.
3
Deals & Transaction Summary in NMD (Last 3 Years)

Directly applicable to NMD clinical site identification and patient recruitment in China.
