Hillgene Biopharma

👤 Dr. Dan Zhang · Co-founder & CEO
1
Executive Summary
▲

Hillgene Biopharma is a China-based cell therapy CDMO headquartered in Suzhou, with GMP-certified facilities in Shenzhen, Shanghai, Belgium, and a facility under development in North Carolina. The company provides end-to-end manufacturing services for CAR-T, CAR-NK, and stem cell-based therapeutics, and has developed a dedicated large-scale stem cell production platform — HiCellx® Stem — alongside closed-process automation and lentiviral vector systems. Hillgene does not develop proprietary therapeutics but has NMPA IND experience with CAR-NK products and proven stem cell GMP production capabilities, which makes it a relevant touchpoint.

Key capabilities relevant to ENCell include the HiCellx® Stem dedicated large-scale stem cell GMP production platform, NMPA IND experience with cell therapy products, and a global GMP footprint spanning Suzhou, Shenzhen, Shanghai, Belgium, and North Carolina (in development).

Fosun Pharma

👤 Xiao Le · Chief Investment Officer
1
Executive Summary
▲

Fosun Pharma (600196.SH; 02196.HK) has built a targeted neuroscience portfolio spanning rare neurogenetic diseases (luvometinib, China's first domestic MEK inhibitor approved for pediatric NF1), neurodegeneration (AR1001 and sodium oligomannate for Alzheimer's). Neurodegenerative disease was designated one of Fosun's three core therapeutic pillars in its 2025 Annual Results.

2
Pipeline / Business Model Introduction
▲
  • Luvometinib (FCN-159 / Fuminning): Self-developed oral MEK1/2 inhibitor. NMPA-approved May 2025 for pediatric NF1-associated plexiform neurofibromas (aged 2+) and Langerhans cell histiocytosis — China's first domestic MEK inhibitor. Received breakthrough therapy designation and priority review from NMPA. Phase III for adult NF1; Phase II for low-grade glioma.
  • AR1001: Oral PDE5 inhibitor for Alzheimer's disease, licensed from NeuCo United (July 2025). Global Phase 3 in 13 countries (1,500 patients); China arm: 126 patients across 25 sites.
  • Sodium Oligomannate Capsule: NMPA-conditionally approved for mild-to-moderate Alzheimer's (2019). Acquired via Green Valley Pharma acquisition (Dec 2025, RMB 1.412B for 51%). Post-marketing confirmatory trials ongoing for re-registration.
  • Ongentys (opicapone): Once-daily COMT inhibitor for Parkinson's motor fluctuations. In clinical use at Boao Lecheng Pilot Zone under special access policies.
3
Deals & Transaction Summary
▲
  • Jul 2025 – AR1001 License-in (NeuCo United): Exclusive license for mainland China/HK/Macau; CNY 40M upfront + milestones + low double-digit royalties; up to CNY 150M total. NeuCo United had previously acquired Greater China rights to AR1001 from Korea's AriBio for approximately KRW 1.02 trillion (~USD 733 million). Fosun also holds an option to expand commercialization rights to Southeast Asia.
  • Dec 2025 – Green Valley Pharma Acquisition: RMB 1.412B for 51% stake (valued RMB 1.674B), adding sodium oligomannate for Alzheimer's.
  • Dec 2018 – DAXXIFY License-in (Revance): Exclusive rights for China/HK/Macau — enabled NMPA approvals for glabellar lines (Sep 2024) and cervical dystonia (2024).
4
Corporate Strategy on Neurology Disease
▲

Fosun is pursuing a multi-modal neurology strategy: pharmacological (luvometinib, AR1001, sodium oligomannate), device-based (MRgFUS via Fosun Insightech for essential tremor and Parkinson's), and specialty access (Ongentys at Boao Lecheng Pilot Zone). Chairman Chen Yuqing named neurodegenerative diseases as one of three core therapeutic priorities (alongside oncology and immune-inflammatory). CEO Liu Yi articulated intent to build a full-disease-course Alzheimer's portfolio — AR1001 for early-stage, sodium oligomannate for mild-to-moderate. Rare disease regulatory experience: luvometinib received breakthrough therapy designation and priority review from NMPA — directly relevant to CMT's regulatory pathway.

CARsgen Therapeutics

👤 Peng Zang · BD VP
1
Executive Summary
▲

CARsgen Therapeutics (2171.HK) is a Shanghai-headquartered CAR T-cell therapy company founded in 2014, with end-to-end capabilities spanning target discovery, preclinical research, clinical development, and commercial-scale GMP manufacturing. CARsgen obtained China's first Drug Production License for CAR-T at its Jinshan facility and commercialized zevor-cel (CT053) — an autologous BCMA CAR-T — approved by NMPA in February 2024 for relapsed/refractory multiple myeloma. zevor-cel is included in China's Commercial Health Insurance Innovative Drug Catalogue, establishing an early reimbursement pathway for cell therapies. FY2025 revenue ~RMB 125.7M; net loss narrowed ~87% YoY. Cash ~RMB 1,123M projected sufficient through 2030. Satri-cel (CT041), world's first Claudin18.2 CAR-T and potential first-in-class for solid tumors, has NDA accepted with Priority Review by NMPA, expected to be approved in 2026. CARsgen's pipeline includes 12 CAR-T products (4 autologous, 7 allogeneic, and 1 in vivo).

2
Corporate Strategy
▲

With dual China–U.S. manufacturing footprints, a vertically integrated GMP production system, and commercial partnerships with domestic leaders such as Huadong Medicine, CARsgen is building an open ecosystem that is "In China, for global markets." Core strategy: establish cash flow through autologous products (zevor-cel), solve accessibility and cost bottlenecks via an allogeneic platform, and achieve pipeline leadership across both solid and hematological tumors.

2
Pipeline / Business Model Introduction
▲

2024 China revenue was CHF 5.328 billion (pharma CHF 2.926B + diagnostics CHF 2.402B). Neurology is one of Roche's three shared priority disease areas, alongside oncology and cardiovascular-metabolic. Global neurology revenue grew 13% in 2024, driven mainly by Ocrevus, Evrysdi, and Elevidys.
